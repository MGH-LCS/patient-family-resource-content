# Who's Who on the Medical Team Taking Care of My Child?

Welcome to the MassGeneral Hospital _for_ Children (MCH_f_C). You and your child's doctors, nurses, social workers and therapists make up the healthcare team, with you as the most important member. The information below will help you understand the different types of doctors on the "Medical Team" that will be involved in your child's care.

# Attending Physician

This is the doctor who leads the medical team that plans the care and treatment for your child.

This doctor will work closely with you to make decisions about your child's care. He/she is ultimately responsible for the medical care provided by the team.

Your child's Attending Physician will either be:

- A Pediatrician who specializes in Hospital Medicine called a "Hospitalist"

- A Subspecialist Pediatrician, who has a specialty in a certain area of pediatrics (such as a Pediatric Pulmonologist who specializes in children's lung problems).

- A physician from your child's primary care practice.

If you are not sure who you child's Attending Physician is, please ask us.

## Consulting Physician

This is a Pediatrician who has a specialty in a specific area (same as Subspecialist) and is called in to "consult" on your child's case. This specialist will work with the medical team to diagnose, treat, and care for your child. He/she may also work with a **Pediatric Fellow**, who is a doctor that has completed medical residency training in Pediatrics and is now in training to become a subspecialist (e.g., Pediatric Pulmonologist). The Consulting Physician and Fellow will make recommendations to the Attending Physician and resident team.

## Resident Physician or House Officer

This is a doctor who has graduated from medical school and is now in a resident training program to become a Pediatrician. A Pediatric resident program usually takes 3 years to complete and Residents are referred to by the year they are currently in.

- First year residents are also called **"Interns".** An intern will examine your child every day and is usually the first person called if you have a question or concern. Your child will have a primary Intern while s/he is in the hospital.

- Second year residents are in between their first and third year. They don't have any other special name.

- Third year residents are also known as **"Senior Residents".** They oversee the work of medical interns and medical students.

Residents work with the Attending Physician to give your child the best care possible. The Pediatric floor at MGH has a Resident Physician available 24 hours a day.

## Medical Students

Medical students are in the third and fourth year of medical school. Third year students work with Interns to help care for your child. Fourth year students usually work under the Senior Resident to care for your child.

MGH_f_C is committed to providing you with the highest standard of care. We think this is enhanced by being a Harvard teaching hospital. Sometimes it can be hard to tell who is who in a place bustling with so many pediatric doctors. All of the members of your child's medical team should introduce themselves and their role on the team. **If you are uncertain who a person is, **please ask them to introduce themselves.**
